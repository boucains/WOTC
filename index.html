Executive Summary

I am a disabled veteran who is requesting a hand up, not a hand out. After an injury and layoff in 2009, I am out of personal resources and options. This proposal allows me to attend Epicodus while providing a clear financial mechanism for you to recoup those costs and more.

Upon successful completion, I would become an employee of Epicodus for between 52 and 65 weeks in order for you to receive a Work Opportunity Tax Credit (WOTC). My hourly rate would be between $9.25 and $11.54 per hour, but the effective rate would be somewhat higher as explained below. At some point during this time I would attend a full course of Epicodus training. No health insurance, vacation, or other benefits are expected.

I have a wide range of technical and administrative skills that are of use supporting your busy staff, as well as a lifelong drive to help others.

Cost Recovery

Tuition

Epicodus will lose the $5,000 external revenue from a different student, however the total recovery is far more than the lost tuition. The cost of my tuition would be recovered through payroll deduction. Once 2080 (52 weeks full-time equivalent) hours are paid, the Work Opportunity Tax Credit is recovered as a credit against that tax year’s payroll taxes. The total OJT payment from the Federal Reboot program can be paid in several ways, but is an upfront value between $1,000 and $2,500.

Effective Payroll

Another way to look at the recovery of costs is to show payroll costs for the period of employment. The total cost of payroll, including employer share, is $26,859.58 for 2080 (52 weeks full-time equivalent) hours. That cost is increased by the loss of external tuition revenue and the cost of my tuition. The costs are then reduced by the value of the OJT component ($1,000 - $2,500), the payback of the tuition through payroll deduction, and the value of the Work Opportunity Tax Credit of $9,600. The resulting effective cost of payroll is $21,259.58.

Hourly Wage Discussion

Overview

Mixing training with payroll is complicated. Actual amounts paid vary depending on the value of the Reboot OJT payment and the exact hourly rate. This is an attempt to provide one alternative and show the effective hourly rate you would be paying.

Actual Hours Worked

Attempting to work for you full time while attending class would be unrealistic. I estimate that while in class I could work an additional 10 hours per week. This plan depends on being paid throughout the year at the same rate, however. While this sounds unreasonable, the amount of credits involved make this amount much closer to a fair value. A 40-hour workweek while not in training would be normal.

Effective Hourly Wage (including Employer Tax Portions)

While the actual minimum hourly rate that satisfies the Work Opportunity Tax Credit is $11.54 for a year, that rate is based on a 40-hour week. Because I would only be working a 10-hour week while in training, my effective pay rate would be higher. The effect total payroll is obtaining by the following: (52 weeks * 40 hours) – ((40 hours paid – 10 hours worked) * 27 weeks of class) = 1270 hours worked $21,259.58 the effective year’s payroll cost / 1270 hours worked = $16.74 per hour worked, which includes the employer’s portions of the payroll taxes.

Effective Hourly Wage (not including Employer Tax Portion)

For normal comparisons, hourly rates are based on the employee’s portion, which is not the full amount paid by the employer, but is used for reference. For the purpose of roughing out the job responsibilities, the effective hourly wage excluding the employer share is $14.49. Given that my last paid position paid me $82K plus a 10% key performer bonus, I’m hoping that appropriate tasks are available that align with the tasks normally paid for a position paying $14.49 per hour.
